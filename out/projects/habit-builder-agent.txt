13:T519,<p><img src="/images/projects/habit-builder-agent-architecture.png" alt="Architecture of Vancesca&#x27;s habit builder agent"></p>
<p>Project from the first cohort of the <a href="https://maven.com/alexey-grigorev/from-rag-to-agents">AI Engineering Bootcamp</a>, by <strong>Vancesca Dinh</strong>.</p>
<p>The Habit Builder agent is grounded in data from the Huberman Lab podcast and medical publications. The data pipeline includes: downloading RSS feeds, transcribing audio with <strong>Faster Whisper</strong>, and storing embeddings in a <strong>Qdrant</strong> vector database. For the agent, she implemented a tool that rewrites user queries in three different ways to improve search results. She used <strong>Logfire</strong> for logging and <strong>Pydantic</strong> for structure. A key part of her presentation was the need for <strong>guardrails</strong>—she found the agent would obediently "draw a cute pig" or translate text into Romanian if asked, so she added checks to keep it focused on the intended use.</p>
<p><strong>Tech stack:</strong> RSS, Faster Whisper, Qdrant, query rewriting, Logfire, Pydantic, guardrails.</p>
